Golf is one of the sports that people are struggling to be better. People would love to know how to be like PGA tour players, what they need to work on, and what the nearest way to be a great player is.

Imagine, for a moment, standing on the green, visualizing each swing with the precision of a grandmaster, just like Collin Morikawa did at the illustrious ZOZO Championship in Chiba, Japan. This tournament, born in 2019, he is a Chinese-Japanese American so his last name is from Japan. It was a special tournament for him because he and his ancestors are related to Japan.

In this blog, we will dive into Morikawa’s winning formula and discover what it takes to become a champion on the greens.

Golf is a sport where victory isn’t hurried, where triumph unfolds over days, not minutes. It is unlike the fast-paced courts of basketball or volleyball and demands endurance and laser focus. It’s a game that rewards those who master the art of patience and concentration.

Look at a leaderboard for the tournament and already, you can see the hint of why Morikawa became a champion.

As you see, he had the best round which marked 63, and he was the player who had the lowest score for the final round in the top 10 players.

Also, take a closer look at the top 10 contenders. Most of the players who finished in the top 10 played better than the third round on the last day. From the table, we can understand that you need to finish strong and play hard til the end if you want to win.

Now, let’s get into more detail. What’s the secret sauce behind those jaw-dropping scores? Of course, they need to make many birdies. However, in the PGA tour, all the players have the ability to get birdies. It’s a skill honed to precision. Then, what sets the champions from those who fall short, despite their birdie prowess?

From this data, Morikawa has the most amount of birdies and also fewer bogeys compared with other players. It is important to make fewer mistakes and avoid the worst case in order to beat all players.

Let’s change the angle. Easy question. What do you need to make birdie? It boils down to two essential moves - nailing that approach onto the green, then sinking the putt.

Morikawa’s greens hit number is 53 which ranked third and the average putting is 1.604 which ranked second. Blue dots are the stats for the top 5 players. They are located left top which means they also have the top level of green hits and average putt.

In Morikawa’s arsenal, precision reigns supreme. With a greens hit count of 53, he claims the bronze, showcasing his mastery of the approach game.
